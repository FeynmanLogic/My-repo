About Me

I am Dhruv Kulkarni, a B.Tech student at S.V. National Institute of Technology, Surat, specializing in Computer Architecture and Artificial Intelligence. My focus is on solving the memory wall problem and enhancing transformer models through innovative optimizations.

I am currently a Research Intern at George Mason University, where I am working on unifying software and hardware optimizations to push the boundaries of computational efficiency.

Projects

Here are some of my notable works in Computer Architecture and AI

Transarch

A unified architectural optimization for transformers, integrating prefetching, Processing in Memory, and software-side optimizations. Developed under the guidance of Dr. Sai Manoj of George Mason University.

KANBoost

A novel prefetcher approach using Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks. Developed as part of my final-year project, led by a team of four under the supervision of Dr. Sai Manoj and Prof. R.P. Gohil.

Translearn

Optimization of transformers using Active Learning and Knowledge Distillation techniques, developed under the guidance of Dr. Chandra Prakash at SVNIT, Surat.
